# Open Science OER Toolkit & Open Research Assessment

## Project Overview

The **Open Science OER Toolkit & Open Research Assessment** project develops an Open Educational Resource (OER) toolkit designed to support a cultural shift in research assessment. The initiative is aligned with the principles of the :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} (CoARA) and contributes to broader international efforts to reform how research quality and impact are evaluated.

The toolkit equips:

- Early Career Researchers (ECRs)  
- Principal Investigators (PIs)  
- Research managers and institutional leaders  

with the knowledge, frameworks, and practical tools needed to implement **responsible research assessment** and **open science practices** across diverse research environments.

At its core, the project promotes:

- Recognition of diverse research contributions and career paths  
- Qualitative evaluation approaches  
- Peer review as a central evaluative practice  
- Responsible and contextualised use of metrics  

The toolkit explicitly discourages the inappropriate use of journal- and publication-based indicators (e.g., Journal Impact Factor, h-index) in line with international reform movements.

## Alignment and Impact

This initiative directly supports the commitments of CoARA and aligns with the :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} Recommendation on Open Science.

By fostering responsible, inclusive, and transparent assessment practices, the project:

- Strengthens institutional readiness for open science mandates  
- Supports long-term research culture change  
- Encourages recognition of diverse research outputs and societal contributions  
- Promotes integrity, transparency, and equity in research evaluation
